Queenstown Gardens playground + frisbee golfBig waterfront playground, free disc golf course through the trees. Burns toddler energy with zero cost or booking. Best late morning.
Kiwi Park (Kiwi & Birdlife Park)See real kiwi in the nocturnal house plus a conservation show. Low-key, indoors when it drizzles, perfect for a settle-in day. Gus and Evelyn will love it.
Move the Luge to todayIf energy is good, do Skyline tomorrow as planned, but Wednesday is a quieter option if the kids are restless.

The steamship and farm animalsThe TSS Earnslaw and the sheepdog and farm show at Walter Peak are a hit across every age, especially Gus and Evelyn.

Fri 3 JulGibbston + split day
Split plan: Dads take the morning (golf or an activity, TBC). Mums get the afternoon free, the Gibbston spa idea sits here. With a 3 and 5 year old, the cleanest version is: one parent per family covers the kids around nap time while the other pairs off, then swap across the weekend.
  • AM: Dads free time.

Options

Glenorchy day trip45 min along a stunning lake road to the head of Lake Wakatipu. The Glenorchy Animal Experience (feed the horses, alpacas, deer) is brilliant for all the kids, and the bakery is worth the drive. A real highlight, but only if everyone is rested.
Otherwise, do nothingThis is a rest day for a reason. A lazy morning, the playground, an early night before pack-up. Protect it.
Mon 6 JulArrowtown + drive to WanakaEasy
BOOKED: Ski gear fitting, Racers Edge, 99 Ardmore Street, Wanaka (03 443 7882). Open 7:30am to 7:30pm daily.
  • AM: check out of the Queenstown hotels.
  • Arrowtown on the way.
  • 12:30pm lunch at Mora Winery.
  • The bakery, the main street, the Chinese Settlement (free, the tiny huts are a kid favourite), the Lakes District Museum, Patagonia Chocolates and the Remarkable Sweet Shop.
  • PM: drive to Wanaka, ski gear fitting at Racers Edge.
Winter drive note: Queenstown to Wanaka is about 1 hour over the Crown Range, the highest sealed road in NZ. It ices up and can close in bad weather. Check the road status that morning. The longer route via Cromwell (about 1h15) is the safe fallback if the Crown Range is closed.

Options

Lake Hayes loop, only the short bitFive minutes from Arrowtown, a pretty lakeside walk. With little legs and gear-fitting to come, do 20 minutes of it, not the full 2-3 hour loop.
Fit gear with hungry kids fed firstGear fitting with four kids takes time. Feed them in Arrowtown so the Racers Edge stop is calm.
Drive the Crown Range in daylightLeave Arrowtown with time to cross before the light goes around 5pm. Stop at the Crown Range summit lookout if clear.

Options

Lay out everyone's gear tonightBoots, layers, gloves, goggles, sunscreen by the door. Morning faff with four kids is the enemy of a good ski day.
Kai Whakapai or The Cow for dinnerBoth easy, warm and central. Save the nicer rooms for a sitter night later in the week.

Age split: Leo, Livvy and Evelyn into group lessons (Cardrona group ski lessons start at age 3). Gus at 3.5 can do a short under-5s lesson or Ski Kindy, or pure snow play with one parent. See the Ski Plan tab.

Options

Puzzling WorldWanaka's iconic family attraction. Illusion rooms and the big outdoor maze. All ages, easily a couple of hours. The classic rest-day-but-still-fun choice.
National Transport & Toy Museum600+ vehicles and tens of thousands of toys near the airport. Surprisingly good for both Leo and the littlies.
Snow Farm tubing, if they want more snowGentle tubing slope, ideal for Gus and first-timers, without the intensity of the ski field.
Lakefront + the Wanaka TreeFree. The Dinosaur Park playground and a flat lakefront stroll to the famous tree.
Sat 11 JulDads' Treble Cone day
  • Dads: Treble Cone, bigger mountain, steeper, more advanced terrain.
  • Kids: rest day or a final snow-play session.
  • ~12:30pm Lunch at the historic Cardrona Hotel, with the Bra Fence photo stop on the ski road (to be confirmed)
Split plan: Treble Cone is genuinely advanced with little beginner terrain, so it suits confident skiers only, the dads. The mums and kids get an easy Wanaka day. Swap a mums' half-day at Cardrona earlier in the week so it is fair.

Options

Treble Cone for the views as much as the runsThe Motatapu basin and Lake Wanaka views from the top are the best on this side of the island. Clear-day pick.
Kids' day: Cardrona Distillery on the way, or townIf the mums fancy it, the historic Cardrona Hotel and distillery sit right on the road. Otherwise an easy town day.
Sun 12 JulTravel to Marsden LakeEasy
  • AM: return ski gear, check out of accommodation.
  • Drive to Marsden Lake (Cromwell). Easy day, wind down after the ski week.
Winter drive note: Wanaka to Cromwell / Marsden Lake is about 50 minutes to 1 hour on the main road, easy and lower-altitude. No Crown Range to worry about on this leg.

Options

Return gear first, then coffeeGet the Racers Edge return done at opening so the drive is unhurried. Pembroke Patisserie croissants for the road.

Cromwell / Marsden Lake · Sun 12 Jul to Tue 14 Jul · 2 nights

Cromwell / Marsden Lake conditions

Sunrise~8:12am
Sunset~5:25pm
Daylight~9 hrs
Daytime~5°C high
Overnight~ -4°C, hard frost
Morningsfreezing basin fog
WeatherHard frosts and freezing fog can linger to midday, then clear to dry, brilliant sun. The warmest, driest corner once the fog lifts.
Best time outA slow start while the fog lifts, then one outing late morning to mid-afternoon.
AccommodationBoth families: Marsden Lake Resort
Sun 12 JulArrive Marsden LakeRest
  • Arrive, settle in, unpack.
  • Relax, quiet evening. Dinner at Moorings on-site, ~6:00pm, so nobody has to drive (to be confirmed).

Options

Resort mini-golf and playgroundOn-site, low-key, lets the kids run after the drive without leaving the grounds.
Moorings for dinnerAward-winning and on-site. The no-logistics option after a travel day is the right call.
Mon 13 JulRest & exploreRest
  • Free day. Explore the local area gently, rest before the trip home.
  • ~10:30am Highlands Motorsport Park, go-karts and the car museum, hot laps for the adults (to be confirmed)
Split plan: this is the natural sitter night. Bannockburn wineries are 10 minutes away, so the adults can do a long lunch or dinner while the kids stay at the resort. Or do a family outing midday and keep the evening quiet.

Options

Lake Dunstan trail, the easy bitFlat walking or biking right at the resort. Do a short out-and-back, not the whole trail.
Highlands Motorsport ParkGo-karts, mini-golf and a genuinely good car museum. Leo's highlight. Hot laps in a V8 are the adults' thrill if anyone wants it.
Cromwell Heritage Precinct + the giant fruitRestored gold-rush buildings by the lake and the famous big-fruit photo at the town entrance. Quick, free, charming.
Bannockburn wineries (sitter night)Mt Difficulty or Carrick for a long lunch with mountain views. The region's best.
Tue 14 JulDepartureTravel
  • Check out, drive to the airport, fly home.
  • Allow extra time for the rental car return.
Winter drive note: Cromwell to Queenstown Airport is about 45 minutes to 1 hour via the Kawarau Gorge, a normal sealed road but allow buffer for ice and gear return. Build in a comfortable margin with kids.

Options

Leave earlier than feels necessaryGear return plus four kids plus winter roads. A relaxed airport beats a stressed one.
Last coffee and fruit at Jackson Orchards7km out on the way, a nice final Central Otago stop if you have the window.
